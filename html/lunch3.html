Crispy Tofu Sisig

Servings: 5

Crispy Tofu Sisig is a plant-based version of the popular Filipino dish Sisig. Traditionally, sisig is made with chopped pork, often including parts like the pig's face, ears, and liver, and it's served sizzling on a hot plate with onions, chili, and seasonings. The crispy tofu version substitutes the pork with tofu, usually deep-fried or pan-fried to achieve a crispy texture.

Ingredients

Ingredients:

  • Extra Firm Tofu (14 oz or ~400g)
  • Chili (5 pieces)
  • Long Green Pepper (2 pieces)
  • Medium Yellow Onion (1 piece)
  • Vegetable Broth (10 tablespoons or ~150ml)
  • Cornstarch (1 teaspoon)
  • Onion Powder (1/2 teaspoon)
  • Soy Sauce (1 tablespoon)
  • Cooking Oil (1.5 cups or ~360ml)
  • Salt and Ground Black Pepper

Instructions

  1. Fry the Tofu:
    • Heat 1½ cups of cooking oil in a pot over medium heat.
    • Once the oil is hot, deep-fry the cubed tofu until golden brown and crispy.
    • Remove the tofu using a kitchen strainer and place it on a plate lined with paper towels. Set aside.
  2. Prepare the Sauce:
    • In a bowl, combine vegetable broth, soy sauce, onion powder, and cornstarch.
    • Stir well until the mixture is smooth and set aside.
  3. Sauté the Aromatics:
    • In a clean pan, heat 3 tablespoons of oil (scooped from the frying pot).
    • Sauté chopped onion, long green peppers, and Thai chili until fragrant.
  4. Combine the Ingredients:
    • Add the crispy fried tofu to the pan and stir-fry for 1 minute.
    • Pour in the broth mixture, tossing everything until well coated.
    • Season with salt and ground black pepper to taste.
  5. Serve:
    • Transfer to a serving plate.
    • Serve hot with a dipping sauce made of soy sauce, calamansi, and crushed chili peppers.
