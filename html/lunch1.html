Pork Monggo

Servings: 4

Pork Monggo (also known as Monggo Guisado) is a Filipino dish made with mung beans (monggo) and pork. It typically consists of cooked mung beans, which are sautéed with pork (often ground pork or pork belly), onions, garlic, tomatoes, and sometimes other vegetables. The dish is usually flavored with fish sauce, soy sauce, and sometimes a bit of bagoong (fermented shrimp paste) for added depth.

Ingredients

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup mung beans (soaked overnight)
  • 200g ground pork
  • 1 small onion (chopped)
  • 1 clove garlic (minced)
  • 1 medium tomato (chopped)
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce (patis)
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 handful of kangkong (optional)
  • 1 cup water

Instructions

  1. Heat oil in a pot. Sauté onion and garlic until fragrant.
  2. Add ground pork and cook until browned.
  3. Drain soaked mung beans and add to the pot with water.
  4. Bring to a boil, then simmer for 30-40 minutes until beans are tender.
  5. Add fish sauce, pepper, and tomato. Simmer for a few more minutes.
  6. Add kangkong during the last 5 minutes (optional).
  7. Enjoy hot with rice.
