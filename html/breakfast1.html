Oyakodon (Chicken and Egg Rice Bowl)

Servings: 2

Oyakodon (親子丼) is a traditional Japanese dish that consists of a rice bowl topped with chicken, eggs, and onions, all simmered together in a savory sauce made from soy sauce, dashi (Japanese soup stock), and mirin (sweet rice wine). The name "oyakodon" literally means "parent and child bowl," which refers to the use of both chicken (the parent) and egg (the child) in the dish

Ingredients

Ingredients

  • Chicken: 200g, skinless and boneless (thighs or breast)
  • Eggs: 2 large
  • Dashi Stock: 1 cup
  • Alternative: 1 cup water mixed with ½ teaspoon seasoning granules
  • Green Onions: for garnish (optional)

Pantry Staples (assumed to be available):

  • Rice: 1 cup uncooked
  • Mirin: 1 tablespoon (optional)
  • Alternative: Mix 1 teaspoon sugar with 1 teaspoon vinegar
  • Sugar: 1 teaspoon

Instructions

  1. Cook the Rice:
    • Rinse the rice under cold water until clear.
    • Cook according to package instructions or using a rice cooker.
  2. Prepare the Chicken and Onion:
    • In a pan, combine dashi stock (or water with seasoning), soy sauce, mirin (or its substitute), and sugar. Bring to a simmer.
    • Add the sliced onion and cook for about 3 minutes until softened.
  3. Add Chicken:
    • Add chicken pieces to the pan and simmer for about 5 minutes until fully cooked.
  4. Add Eggs:
    • Lightly beat the eggs and pour over the chicken and onion mixture.
    • Cover and cook for about 2 minutes until the eggs are just set but still slightly runny.
  5. Serve:
    • Divide the cooked rice into two bowls.
    • Spoon the chicken and egg mixture over the rice.
    • Garnish with chopped green onions if desired.
